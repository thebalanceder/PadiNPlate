# Category 6: Plantation Method

Method selection affects labor cost, seed rate, weed pressure, and yield potential.

## Plantation Methods

### 1. Transplanting
**Traditional method**
- seedlings grown in nursery
- manually transplanted to main field
- Better stand establishment
- Lower seed rate
- Higher labor requirement

### 2. Direct Seeding
**Broadcast or line seeding**
- Seeds sown directly in flooded or dry field
- Lower labor requirement
- Higher seed rate
- Increased weed pressure
- Faster establishment

### 3. Mechanical Transplanting
**Using mechanical transplanter**
- Requires seedling mat preparation
- Consistent spacing
- Medium labor requirement
- Good stand establishment

## Recommendation Parameters

### Seed Rate
| Method | Seed Rate (kg/ha) |
|--------|-------------------|
| Transplanting | 20-30 |
| Direct seeding (broadcast) | 80-120 |
| Direct seeding (line) | 60-80 |
| Mechanical transplanting | 25-35 |

### Spacing Recommendations
- Row spacing: 20-25 cm
- Plant spacing within row: 15-20 cm
- Optimal plant density: 25-30 plants/m²

### Labor Requirements
| Activity | Man-days/Hectare |
|----------|------------------|
| Land preparation | 15-20 |
| Nursery preparation | 5-8 |
| Transplanting | 25-35 |
| Direct seeding | 8-12 |
| Weeding (manual) | 20-30 |
| Harvesting | 15-25 |

## Method Comparison

| Factor | Transplanting | Direct Seeding |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Initial cost | Higher | Lower |
| Labor requirement | High | Low |
| Weed management | Easier | More difficult |
| Crop duration | Shorter (less maturity time) | Slightly longer |
| Yield potential | Equal to slightly higher | Equal |
| Risk | Lower | Higher (weeds, bird damage) |
